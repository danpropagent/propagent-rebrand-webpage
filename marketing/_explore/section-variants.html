A4

Pain symptom catalog

Named failure modes — not phases. Each card: the symptom, what it costs, how Propagent neutralizes it.

From RFP to response

The five failure modes we hear every pursuit week.

These aren't phases. They're symptoms that show up as pure friction — and they cost real bids. Each is the kind of thing principals only talk about over coffee, not in the methodology slide.

The 8-day blind spot

RFPs publish to portals while nobody's watching. By the time BD spots one, half the runway is already gone.

Cost Average 6 days of pursuit runway lost before the team starts.
Propagent Bid Scout watches every relevant portal and flags within an hour of publication. Scored against firm fit before the team sees it.

The handoff blackout

Capture writes the storyline. PM picks it up days later. By then the angle has drifted and rebuilding it eats more time than writing it did.

Cost 1–2 days lost rediscovering decisions that were already made.
Propagent The storyline lives in the system from first qualification. The PM picks it up where it is, not where it was.

The midnight compliance matrix

Required by the proposal manager at 11pm before submit. Built as an artifact of panic, not coverage.

Cost Late-night work, missed requirements, defensible only to a point.
Propagent Compliance is a continuous artifact, populated as the team works. Submit-ready by day three.

The PTO-stricken reviewer

Reviewer with a 24-hour turnaround can't read 80 pages. So they read three, sign off, and pray.

Cost Surface-level review. Missed risks. Errors that should have been caught.
Propagent Reviewers get a one-page provenance summary. Click through to sources only where they want to verify.

The boilerplate that doesn't fit

Last pursuit's win themes copied forward into this one. Generic-sounding. Embarrassing in debrief.

Cost Lower win rate on competitive bids; "they didn't really read it" feedback.
Propagent Themes are built from this pursuit's qualification data — not last month's draft.
